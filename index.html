Why Usual Apps Fail in Korea?

Translation apps and vegan directories cannot protect you from 'structural risks' in Korean dining culture.

Vegan ≠ Safe

Korean vegan food often uses soy, wheat, peanuts, and sesame for flavor. 'No Meat' does not mean 'Allergy-Free'.

Lost in Context

Translators only swap words. "No shrimp" is often misunderstood as "Just remove shrimp from the soup", ignoring cross-contamination.

Hidden Ingredients

Busy staff may not know about hidden broth (anchovy/shrimp) or secret sauces. "I don't think so" often means "I don't know".
